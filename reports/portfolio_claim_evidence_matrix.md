## Additional limitations to preserve in public materials

- Runtime evidence was collected locally on an NVIDIA RTX 4090; do not imply production-scale infrastructure or cloud cost generalization.
- The workflow is semi-automatic and human-gated, not a production MLOps service.
- AFHQ final selection used quick-200; full-1000, EMA selection, sampler ablation and CFG sweep were deliberately skipped.
- AFHQ early-stop improves FID/KID but gives up precision/recall versus the frozen baseline; present the trade-off explicitly.
- Large checkpoints, datasets, caches and full evaluation outputs are intentionally excluded from Git; reports and hashes point to local artifacts.
- The redundant AFHQ quick-200 verification run used the same fixed protocol and is not an independent statistical replicate.

## Writer checklist

1. Keep dataset, resolution, step count, sampler, seed range and sample count beside every metric table.
2. Label cross-step comparisons and partial runs in the sentence containing the claim.
3. Link to a report and, where useful, a small representative grid under `docs/assets/`.
4. Keep the token observation in an “operational notes / next instrumentation” subsection, never in the ML results headline.
